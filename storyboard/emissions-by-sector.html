Global Warming and CO2 Emissions: A Data-Driven Story

Global CO2 emissions have risen consistently since 1990. Electricity and heat production show the steepest increase among all sectors, followed by transportation and industrial emissions. Agriculture and waste management sectors display more modest but steady growth in emissions.

China, India, and the United States are the top three global emitters, together producing nearly half of global CO2 emissions. China leads in industrial and electricity emissions, India in agricultural emissions, and the United States in transportation and building-related emissions. Singapore shows exceptionally high aviation emissions, ranking among the world’s top aviation emission sources.

Data:  Hannah Ritchie, Pablo Rosado and Max Roser (2023) - “CO₂ and Greenhouse Gas Emissions”
