CO2 Emissions & Global Temperature Change

Introduction

This chart shows the relationship between global carbon dioxide emissions and temperature changes since the year 1880. The CO2 emission represents cumulative emissions of carbon dioxide from fossil fuel and industry in the certain region. The temperature change represents the change in global mean surface temperature caused by greenhouse effect. The data is illustrated with a biaxial scatter plot.

From the graph, we can see that carbon dioxide emissions have shown a significant upward trend over the past 140 years. At the beginning of the Industrial Revolution, emissions were very low. Growth in emissions was still relatively slow until the mid-20th century. In the second half of the 20th century, with the rapid development of the global economy, industry and transportation scale, carbon dioxide emissions increased sharply.

It can also be seen that global temperatures have shown a clear upward trend over the past century. Especially in the early 21st century, the rate of temperature rise seems to be accelerating, further raising concerns about climate change.This trend is closely related to the rise in global CO2 emissions, reflecting the impact of CO2 on climate change.

Generally, the chart shows the impact of human activities on climate change since the Industrial Revolution, particularly the use of fossil fuels and the increase in greenhouse gas emissions caused by modern industrialization processes.

Carbon dioxide emissions are the primary driver of global climate change. It's widely recognized that to avoid the worst impacts of climate change, the world needs to urgently reduce emissions. In future, the world must reach an agreement about how this responsibility is shared between regions, countries, and individuals, and take solid actions ASAP.
